Course Overview

Hi, I'm Akif and I have a lot of experience doing competitive programming, but more importantly teaching and guiding people to become better at it through the Rugers CP Club. In this course I want to take you through a distilled version of the same syllabus and topics that my team learned to become ICPC World Finalists.

Learning algorithms and data structures from the harsh and unforgiving standpoint of competitive programming will force you to know these topics like the back of your hand. They won't be something that you learn at a theoretic level and vaguely remember — you have to become intimately familar with them to have any hope of solving a problem with them. And you'll have an expert personally guiding you to that deep understanding.

Additionally, perhaps more significantly, competitive programming very rapidly develops your problem solving and mathematical maturity, especially related to computer science. I will walk you through various thinking patterns and approaches that will make you a much better coder.

Course Topics

We will go over these topics in approximately the order below, but I will tailor them to the interest and progress of the student. Each of the two courses (which are broken up the same way we do it for RUCP students) are intended to take somewhere around a month to two. Progression requirements are based on Codeforces rating, and inidicated below. The beginner course focuses more on practice and intution building, so the advanced course covers topics faster — both should take the same time in theory.

Practice will happen on Codeforces (and will happen partially on my time and partially on the students time). The problems are drawn from the practice contests that RUCP ICPC teams use (but again tailored to the student).
